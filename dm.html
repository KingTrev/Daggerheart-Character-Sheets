DICE & ROLLS
ACTION ROLL — STEP BY STEP
1. Announce action + trait used
2. GM sets Difficulty (or it's stated)
3. Roll 2d12 (Hope Die + Fear Die) + Trait
4. Compare total to Difficulty
5. Determine Hope or Fear result
DUALITY DICE RESULTS
SUCCESS WITH HOPE
Total ≥ Difficulty, Hope Die higher. Succeed + gain 1 Hope.
SUCCESS WITH FEAR
Total ≥ Difficulty, Fear Die higher. Succeed, GM gains 1 Fear.
FAILURE WITH HOPE
Total < Difficulty, Hope Die higher. Fail + minor consequence + gain 1 Hope.
FAILURE WITH FEAR
Total < Difficulty, Fear Die higher. Fail + major consequence, GM gains 1 Fear.
Critical Success: Matching dice — auto succeed with bonus, gain Hope, clear Stress. Attack = critical damage (add max dice result).
REACTION ROLL
Made in response to an attack or hazard. Works like an action roll but does not generate Hope or Fear and doesn't trigger GM moves. Critical success ignores effects without clearing Stress/gaining Hope.
GROUP ACTION ROLL
One PC leads (action roll), others make reaction rolls with appropriate traits. Lead gets +1 per success, −1 per failure on the reaction rolls.
TAG TEAM ROLL
Once per session per PC — spend 3 Hope to initiate. Both PCs roll separately, choose which result to apply. On Hope: all PCs gain Hope. On Fear: GM gains Fear per PC. Successful attack: both roll damage, add totals.

PROFICIENCY & DAMAGE
Proficiency = number of damage dice rolled. Lv 1–4: 1 | Lv 5–7: 2 | Lv 8–10: 3
Unarmed: [Proficiency]d4 physical damage. Uses Strength or Finesse (GM's choice).
Critical damage: Roll damage as normal, then add the maximum possible result of the damage dice to the total.

PLAYER MECHANICS
EVASION
A PC's ability to avoid attacks. Attack rolls against a PC use their Evasion as the Difficulty. Set by class + modifiers. Adversary attacks use adversary's Difficulty score instead.
HIT POINTS & DAMAGE THRESHOLDS
Thresholds = armor base + PC level. When damage is dealt:
MINORBelow Major threshold → mark 1 HP
MAJORAt/above Major, below Severe → mark 2 HP
SEVEREAt/above Severe threshold → mark 3 HP
Damage reduced to 0 or below: no HP marked.
ARMOR SLOTS
Mark 1 Armor Slot to reduce HP marked by 1. Armor Score = base score + bonuses. Cleared during downtime or rest.
STRESS
Mental/physical/emotional strain. When last Stress is marked: become Vulnerable until at least 1 is cleared. Can't mark Stress when full → mark HP instead. Cleared via downtime.
HELPING AN ALLY
Spend 1 Hope to help an ally making an action roll. They gain a +1 bonus to the result of their Hope or Fear Die. Multiple PCs can help the same roll.
CONDITIONS
HIDDENRolls against Hidden have disadvantage. Ends when seen or when you attack.
RESTRAINEDCan't move. Can still take actions from current position.
VULNERABLEAll rolls targeting Vulnerable creature have advantage.
Temporary conditions: PC makes a move (usually action roll) to clear. Adversary: GM puts spotlight on them; they describe clearing it (no roll needed, uses their spotlight).
Spend 1 Fear to end a temporary effect.
COUNTDOWNS

DEATH MOVES
When a PC marks their last HP:
BLAZECharacter embraces death. Describe one last heroic act, then cross through the veil.
AVOIDAvoid death, face consequences. Roll Hope Die: on a 1, gain a scar (cross a Hope slot). If last Hope slot: journey ends.
RISK ITRoll Duality Dice. Hope higher: survive, clear HP/Stress = Hope Die value. Fear higher: cross through veil. Tie: clear all HP and Stress.
DOWNTIME MOVES
Short rest (2 moves): Tend to Wounds (clear 1d4+Tier HP), Clear Stress (1d4+Tier), Repair Armor (1d4+Tier slots), Prepare (Hope + advantage), Work on Project. GM gains 1d4 Fear.
Long rest (2 moves): Tend All Wounds, Clear All Stress, Repair All Armor, Prepare (2 Hope each), Work on Project. GM gains 1d4 + PCs Fear + can advance a long-term countdown.

GM TOOLS
HOPE — PLAYERS SPEND TO
• Add an Experience bonus to a roll
• Help an ally (+1 to their die result)
• Initiate a Tag Team Roll (3 Hope)
• Activate a Hope Feature (usually 3 Hope)
• Recall a spent domain card
Max 6 Hope per PC. Carries over between sessions.
FEAR — GM SPENDS TO
• Steal the spotlight & make a GM move (1 Fear)
• Make an additional GM move (1 Fear)
• Use an adversary's Fear Feature (1 Fear)
• Use an environment's Fear Feature (1 Fear)
• Add adversary Experience to a roll (1 Fear)
• End a temporary condition or effect (1 Fear)
Max 12 Fear. Start campaign with 1 Fear per PC. Gain Fear when PCs roll with Fear or rest.
GM MOVES — WHEN TO MAKE THEM
Make a GM move when a PC rolls with Fear or fails an action roll.
SUCCESS / HOPEYes, and… Player describes success, gains Hope.
SUCCESS / FEARYes, but… Success + consequence, GM gains Fear.
FAILURE / HOPENo, but… Fail + minor consequence, gain Hope.
FAILURE / FEARNo, and… Fail + major consequence, GM gains Fear.
EXAMPLE GM MOVES
• Have a PC mark Stress · Separate the party · Introduce a new threat · Reveal an unwelcome truth · Show the collateral damage · Clear an adversary's condition · Spotlight an adversary · Trigger an environment effect · Advance a countdown · Put someone in a tough spot
Use softer moves on Hope, harder moves on Fear.

IMPROVISING ADVERSARIES
Give them a name and a Difficulty. Adversaries don't need HP/Stress unless dramatically appropriate. Base moves on their motives. Don't need stat blocks — put spotlight on what they do, show how the world responds. Spend Fear to escalate.
GM PRINCIPLES
• Portray a rich and dangerous world
• Fill the world with interesting characters
• Play to find out what happens
• Address the characters, not the players
• Embrace failure as a story tool
• Telegraph danger before it strikes
• Be a fan of the player characters
• Spend Fear — don't hoard it
• Make the world feel consequential
